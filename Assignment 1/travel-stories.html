Eiffel Tower

The Eiffel Tower, an iron marvel soaring 330 meters above Paris, has been a global icon since its completion in 1889. Originally criticized by some of France’s leading artists, it has become the city’s beloved symbol of romance and innovation. Visitors can climb its levels for breathtaking panoramic views of the Seine River and the historic cityscape, especially magical at sunset when the tower lights up and sparkles every hour on the hour.

Colosseum

The Colosseum in Rome is one of the world’s most impressive ancient monuments. Completed in 80 AD, this massive amphitheater could hold over 50,000 spectators who gathered to watch gladiatorial battles, animal hunts, and dramatic reenactments. Today, the Colosseum stands as a powerful reminder of the grandeur and complexity of Roman engineering and culture, attracting millions who walk through its ancient corridors to imagine the echoes of history within its stone walls.

Statue of Liberty

The Statue of Liberty rises proudly from Liberty Island in New York Harbor, greeting travelers and immigrants since 1886 as a universal symbol of freedom and democracy. Designed by French sculptor Frédéric Auguste Bartholdi and gifted by France to the United States, Lady Liberty holds her torch high, lighting the way to a better future. A visit offers the chance to climb to her crown, explore the museum inside the pedestal, and admire stunning views of Manhattan’s skyline and Ellis Island nearby.
